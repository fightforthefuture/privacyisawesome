Privacy is awesome.

Because...

Privacy means you can be yourself. You can express ideas without fear of being discriminated against or unjustly punished. It's your life without pausing to think who's watching: party hard, open up to a friend, organize a revolution, hang out with whoever you want--privacy keeps you safe. It's a core principle in any free society.

But...

Congress is moving bills (CISPA, Lieberman-Collins/Cyber Security Act) that would end meaningful privacy. Your texts, your emails.... shared by corporations, in the hands of government with scant limits. Goodbye privacy, hello police state. We need to kill their plans.

Take 5 steps to kill CISPA, the Cyber Security Act and the SECURE IT Act and save privacy

We can kill CISPA, the Cyber Security Act and the SECURE IT Act like we killed SOPA, but we've got just one shot -- Memorial Day recess. The Senate votes on the bill in early June. That doesn't give us much time, but if thousands of people call now, we can win. In short, call the Senate in droves, meet with them. We beat CISPA, the Cyber Security Act and the SECURE IT Act.

5/28 - 6/1 - Senate votes in early June

Call your senators, tell them you oppose Lieberman-Collins, and ask for a meeting or information about an event during the Memorial Day recess. This tells Senators that their constituents strongly oppose the bill. And it's the first step to getting a constituent meeting, perhaps the most powerful way to change a Senator's mind (if you can't do a meeting, no problem, others can--just get a date and time!)

Enter your phone number and we'll connect you to your Senator. We'll give you a script, numbers to call, and a form to report back (if you get a meeting, or if they say something useful).
